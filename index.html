Why Foresight, Why Now

The packaging industry is at an inflection point. Regulatory pressure (the EU PPWR), material innovation, AI-driven manufacturing, and M&A consolidation, including the International Paper-DS Smith combination, are reshaping the landscape simultaneously. These forces are not independent; they interact and amplify each other in ways that traditional competitive intelligence struggles to capture.

Traditional market intelligence is reactive: it tells you what happened. Strategic foresight is proactive: it helps leadership anticipate shifts 12-36 months out and position the business ahead of competitors. In an industry where investment cycles are long and switching costs are high, the organisations that see around corners will win.

Key Design Principles

  • Embedded, not isolated: Foresight must sit close to strategy, not in a silo. Regular touchpoints with commercial, R&D, and public affairs teams
  • Technology-enabled: GenAI tools reduce manual effort by 60-70%, allowing a small team to cover broad terrain across geographies and topics
  • Output-oriented: Every foresight output must answer "So What?" and connect to a decision or action. No insight without implication
  • Cadence-driven: Weekly signal briefs, monthly theme deep-dives, quarterly strategic scenarios, annual Foresight Summit

Methodology

Data collection: Python-based automated scanning of Google News RSS feeds using 12 curated search queries. Articles are deduplicated by normalised headline.

Source quality filter: Only articles from trusted industry publications (Packaging Dive, Packaging World, Packaging Europe, etc.) and tier-1 business media (Financial Times, Reuters, Bloomberg, etc.) are retained. Articles must match at least 2 theme keywords to qualify.

Strategic Priority Matrix: 15 key trends curated from signal analysis and mapped to an Act / Analyse / Observe framework to guide strategic prioritisation and resource allocation.
